Profile

Project delivery for technical teams and business stakeholders.

Technical delivery gets difficult when requirements, engineering constraints, suppliers, validation paths, and business decisions move at the same time. My work is to turn that ambiguity into a plan people can run, review, and adjust.

That means clear baselines, usable estimates, defined responsibilities, change control, risk visibility, and reporting that technical teams, operations, and executives can act on.

Methods

How technical uncertainty gets turned into decisions.

01 / Investigation flow

Wireless RF integration failure, handled as a controlled investigation.

Built for late-stage integration where bench behavior and field behavior diverge, several technical causes remain plausible, and leadership needs options instead of guesswork.

02 / Schedule baseline

Integrated schedule built around gates, evidence, and decision timing.

The schedule is not a lifecycle poster. It shows the parts of the program that can block each other: supplier interface freeze, firmware integration, RF envelope proof, field validation, and steering decisions.
