Profile

Project delivery for technical teams and business stakeholders.

Technical delivery gets difficult when requirements, engineering constraints, suppliers, validation paths, and business decisions move at the same time. My work is to turn that ambiguity into a plan people can run, review, and adjust.

That means clear baselines, usable estimates, defined responsibilities, change control, risk visibility, and reporting that technical teams, operations, and executives can act on.

Methods

Delivery controls and working outputs.

These examples show the working outputs used to organize and control technical delivery. They define scope, establish baselines, assign responsibilities, monitor risk, and prepare decisions in a form that can be reviewed without exposing client material.

01 / Operating model

Delivery as a controlled cycle.

Work progresses through defined steps. At each step, decisions are issued, status and evidence are collected, and changes are reviewed against the baseline.

The top rail represents decisions. The bottom rail represents status, evidence, and risk reporting.

02 / PM artifact register

Common project controls.

The register lists typical deliverables used to manage scope, schedule, risk, responsibilities, communications, dependencies, and acceptance. The set is adjusted to the size and governance needs of the program.

01Scope baseline

WBS, deliverable tree, exclusions

02Schedule baseline

milestones, dependencies, gates

03RAID register

risks, assumptions, issues, dependencies

04Stakeholder register

roles, influence, escalation paths

05Interface map

technical interfaces, blockers, owners

06Reporting cadence

status cycle, steering material, decisions

07Validation plan

evidence, acceptance criteria, sequence

03 / Schedule baseline

Schedule baseline with critical path.

The baseline identifies work packages, dependencies, gates, progress, and review points. The critical path is highlighted to show activities that drive the delivery date.

Bars show progress against the approved baseline. The dashed line marks the current review point. Gold diamonds mark control gates.

04 / Responsibility assignment matrix

Responsibility assignment matrix.

The matrix identifies who is responsible, accountable, consulted, and informed for key activities. It is used to reduce ownership gaps, duplicate effort, and unclear escalation.

RResponsibleAAccountableCConsultedIInformed

05 / Risk heatmap

Probability and impact heat map.

Risks from the RAID register are assessed by probability and impact. The heat map supports prioritization, escalation, and review of mitigation actions.

Each marker is linked to an owner, mitigation action, due date, and reporting cadence.
